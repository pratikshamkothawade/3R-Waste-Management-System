Electronic Waste

In 2020-2021, India processed 3.4 lakh tonnes of e-waste.

Electronic devices seem like they were made to resist decomposition … forever. The glass they might contain takes 1-2 million years to decompose. Plastics last forever: a plastic jug lasts 1 million years, and plastic bags stick around 20 to 1,000 years.


♻ Can we reuse them?

E-waste often contains precious metals such as gold, copper and nickel and rare materials of strategic value such as indium and palladium. These precious and heavy metals could be recovered, recycled and used as valuable source of secondary raw materials.


♻ Reusing steps


  1. 1. Find a local repair shop.
  2. 2. Find a Repair Café.
  3. 3. Trade services with a fix-it friend.
  4. 4. Bring your usable devices to a donation center.
  5. 5. Ask around to groups in your community.
  6. 6. Sell your old electronics locally.
  7. 7. Sell your used devices online.

♻ Reducing steps


  1. 1. Recycle rechargeable batteries when necessary.
  2. 2. Check the product lifespan.
  3. 3. Reuse as often as possible.
  4. 4. Limit your electronics.
  5. 5. Maintain.
  6. 6. Recycle electronics and batteries in e-waste recycling bins located around campus.
  7. 7. Buy environmentally friendly electronics.

♻ Disposing / Decomposing steps


  1. 1. Give Back to Your Electronic Companies and Drop Off Points
  2. 2. Give Your Electronic Waste to a Certified E-Waste Recycler
  3. 3. Visit Civic Institutions
  4. 4. Never Dump E-waste Into The Dustbin
  5. 5. Sort Your E-waste
  6. 6. Dispose of E-waste Responsibly
